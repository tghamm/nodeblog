CSS3 Transforms

November 2, 2014

I've been studying for the MS 70-480 exam (building web apps with HTML5, JavaScript, and CSS3), recently. My employer asked me to take the exam this year, because the more certified developers that the company has, the greater the benefits to the company on MSDN subscriptions and software licenses. I hate standardized tests, and historically, have not done very well. Consequently, I was pretty skeptical that I would get much personal benefit out of the exam prep. However, I have been pleasantly surprised by the many insights I am gaining into the inner-workings of magical frameworks like jQuery, KnockoutJS, and AngularJS.

Since I learn more by doing, than by rote memorization, I've been typing out all of the examples from the study guide that I have been reading. I figure as long as I am doing that, I may as well post some of the examples to the ol' blog along the way.

With that in mind, you will find below some examples of some CSS3 transformations that you can accomplish with just a few lines of code. There's nothing mind-blowing here, but perhaps you might come to understand that one does not always need to rely on some heavy framework to do basic DOM manipulation.

CSS3 Transform Examples
