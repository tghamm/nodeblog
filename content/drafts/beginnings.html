Beginnings, Part 1

****UPDATE PUBLICATION DATE*****

I've been listening to Shawn Wildermuth's Hello World Podcast recently, and it has caused me to think about my own early experiences with computers. Many of the people Shawn has interviewed are about my age, and I have found a lot of commonality between the interviewees' experiences with programming in the early 1980s and my own.

In my "About" page, I refer to my genesis as a programmer somewhere around 1982. I probably had some exposure a year or so before that with a TRS-80 (probably a Model III), to which I had access through my math class in middle school. Today, many people refer to those TRS-80 machines as "Trash 80s", but you will never hear that phrase come out of my mouth. In those early days of the 1980s, those TRS-80 machines with a 5 1/4" floppy drive cost about $1,700 (enough to buy many people a very reliable car back then) and I would have done nearly anything to have one at home. My classmates were only interested in typing swear words and other obscene phrases into the Eliza program to see how she would respond. Admittedly, that was good for quite a few laughs, but I spent many hours during lunch and after school sitting in that classroom and experimenting and writing programs in BASIC. I was alone in this endeavor, which was just fine by me. For me that machine was the Holy Grail.
